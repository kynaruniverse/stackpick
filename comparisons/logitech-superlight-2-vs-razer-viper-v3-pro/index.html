Detailed Breakdown

Weight & Feel in Hand

The Viper V3 Pro is technically lighter at 54g vs 60g, but in practice both feel feather-light during play ‚Äî you won't notice 6g mid-game. What you will notice is the shape. The Superlight 2 uses a classic symmetrical hump profile similar to the original GPX but refined. The Viper V3 Pro has a more pronounced flare at the rear that suits fingertip and claw grippers who like to anchor the palm heel without committing to a full palm grip. If you're coming from an older Viper or DeathAdder, the V3 Pro will feel instantly familiar. The Superlight 2 appeals to players who've been in the Logitech ecosystem or want the flattest, most neutral shape available.

Sensor Comparison

Both sensors are flawless by any objective measure ‚Äî no acceleration, no angle snapping, no jitter at any DPI or speed. The Razer Focus Pro 35K edges out the HERO 2 on paper (35,000 DPI vs 32,000 DPI, slightly better motion sync on hard mats), but real-world performance is identical for 99% of players. If you use a very hard mousepad and push very high speeds, the Focus Pro has a marginal advantage. On cloth pads at normal speeds, they're interchangeable.

Wireless Performance

Both use 2.4GHz wireless with polling rates up to 1000Hz (4,000Hz with firmware updates depending on revision). LIGHTSPEED and HyperSpeed are both around 1ms and considered equivalent to wired by every professional reviewer and pro player who has tested them. Neither will give you wireless disadvantage. The Viper V3 Pro's 95-hour battery significantly beats the Superlight 2's 70-hour figure ‚Äî you'll charge the Razer noticeably less often, which matters if you hate remembering to plug in.

Shape & Grip Style

This is really the deciding factor. The Superlight 2 suits palm and claw grips with medium hands (17-19cm hand length). It's a very safe, neutral shape that works for most people. The Viper V3 Pro suits fingertip, claw, and some palm grippers who prefer the more tapered front-end and flared rear ‚Äî it's particularly good for players with larger hands (18-21cm) who want a bit more rear support than the Superlight 2 provides. Razer's thumb scallop also helps if you like to anchor your thumb low on the mouse.

Software & Ecosystem

If you already use Logitech peripherals with G HUB, the Superlight 2 slots in without friction. If you're on Razer Synapse with a keyboard or headset, the Viper V3 Pro integrates more cleanly. Both apps are mature and do the job. If you're starting fresh, G HUB has a slight edge for stability ‚Äî Synapse has historically had more background process issues on some Windows configurations, though recent versions have improved significantly.
