Quick Answer

Two of the best wireless gaming mice ever made. Both cost similar money, both use flagship sensors, both are used by pro players. This comparison exists to help you decide which one to actually buy.

Detailed Breakdown

Weight ‚Äî Viper V3 Pro wins

The Viper V3 Pro at 54g is genuinely lighter than the Superlight 2 at 60g. In isolation both are ultralight, but if you're specifically chasing the lowest possible weight, Razer wins here. Neither mouse will fatigue your wrist on a full day of gaming.

Sensor ‚Äî Both are flawless, Viper edges on paper

The HERO 2 in the Superlight 2 and the Focus Pro 35K in the Viper V3 Pro are both objectively flawless sensors ‚Äî zero acceleration, zero jitter, perfectly linear tracking at any speed. The Focus Pro has a higher max DPI (35K vs 32K) and supports 4000Hz polling with the HyperPolling dongle. In practice, at the DPI ranges anyone actually uses (400‚Äì3200), you will not feel a difference. Both sensors are used by top-ranked competitive players.

Polling Rate ‚Äî Viper V3 Pro wins on paper

The Viper V3 Pro supports 4000Hz polling with Razer's HyperPolling dongle (sold separately). The Superlight 2 ships at 1000Hz and supports 2000Hz with Logitech's separate adapter. Higher polling means more position updates per second, which reduces input lag. The difference between 1000Hz and 4000Hz is measurable in microseconds ‚Äî real in theory, imperceptible in practice for all but the highest-level pros. If you're not playing at the absolute top of the ladder, 1000Hz is perfectly fine.

Shape ‚Äî comes down to your hand

This is the most important factor and it's personal. The Superlight 2 is a right-hand-only symmetrical shape ‚Äî similar to the G Pro Wireless that many pro players have used for years. It suits medium-to-large hands with a claw or fingertip grip. The Viper V3 Pro is ambidextrous ‚Äî it works for both hands and suits a wider range of grip styles and hand sizes. If you're left-handed, Viper is your only option here. If you've used a G Pro before and loved it, Superlight 2 is the natural upgrade.

Battery Life ‚Äî identical

Both claim approximately 95 hours of battery life at 1000Hz. Real-world use matches this closely. Neither mouse will need charging more than once a week for most gamers. Both charge via USB-C.

Clicks and switches

The Superlight 2 uses Logitech's LIGHTFORCE optical-mechanical hybrid switches ‚Äî essentially optical speed with mechanical feel. They have a satisfying tactile click and are rated for 100M actuations. The Viper V3 Pro uses Razer's Gen-3 optical switches ‚Äî true optical, no debounce delay, also rated for 100M actuations. Both are excellent. The Superlight 2 feels slightly more premium to the touch; the Viper feels crisper and faster.

Software

Logitech G HUB vs Razer Synapse. Both have had stability issues over the years. G HUB has improved significantly. Synapse is more feature-rich but heavier. If you're already using one ecosystem's products, stay there ‚Äî mixing is a headache.

Our Verdict

If you're right-handed and have no strong shape preference, the Superlight 2 is our pick ‚Äî the LIGHTFORCE switches are the best clicks we've felt on a wireless mouse and the HERO 2 sensor is proven across thousands of pro hours. It's the safer recommendation for most people.

If you're left-handed, use a palm grip on larger hands, or are coming from a Razer setup, the Viper V3 Pro is the right call. It's lighter, cheaper, and just as capable at any polling rate you'll realistically use.

You genuinely cannot go wrong with either. Both are endgame mice.
