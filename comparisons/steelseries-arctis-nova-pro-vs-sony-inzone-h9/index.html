Detailed Breakdown

Battery: Hot-Swap System vs Single Battery

This is where the Nova Pro wins outright. It ships with two batteries and a charging case built into the base station. One battery charges while the other runs the headset ‚Äî meaning you will never have to plug in the headset itself. Ever. Swap takes under 10 seconds. The Sony INZONE H9 at 32 hours is genuinely excellent battery life, but once it needs charging you're tethered or waiting. For anyone who sessions for 6+ hours or forgets to charge, the Nova Pro's system is a meaningful quality-of-life win.

Simultaneous Multi-Device Connection

The Nova Pro Wireless can connect to PC via 2.4GHz and console (or phone) via Bluetooth at the same time ‚Äî you hear both simultaneously and can switch audio output without touching the headset. The INZONE H9 supports both 2.4GHz and Bluetooth, but only one at a time. If you frequently switch between PC and phone for calls, or use both PC and console in the same session, the Nova Pro's simultaneous mode is a genuine advantage.

Console Compatibility: Sony's Ecosystem Advantage

The Sony INZONE H9 is natively optimised for PS5. It supports PlayStation's 360 Spatial Sound engine ‚Äî the headset and console communicate to deliver accurate spatial audio in PS5-native titles. The Arctis Nova Pro Wireless, despite being an excellent headset, does not access PS5's native spatial audio system (it works on PS5 but as a generic USB device). If PS5 is your primary platform, this is a real difference for compatible titles.

Sound Quality

Both use 40mm drivers and both sound excellent for gaming. The Nova Pro leans slightly brighter ‚Äî footsteps and high-frequency cues come through clearly. The INZONE H9 has a wider soundstage and a warmer tuning that works well for both gaming and music listening. Neither is dramatically better than the other on pure audio quality ‚Äî this comes down to tuning preference and what software EQ you apply.

ANC (Active Noise Cancellation)

Both have ANC. The Nova Pro's ANC is notably effective ‚Äî it handles consistent background noise (fans, traffic, air conditioning) well. The INZONE H9's ANC is good but doesn't quite match the Nova Pro's consistency. For gaming, ANC matters most in noisy environments; both are sufficient. The Nova Pro wins on ANC quality.

Build Quality and Comfort

The Nova Pro has a steel and aluminum headband, premium feel, and a reputation for lasting years. The INZONE H9 is lighter at 324g vs 338g, uses a plastic and rubber construction, and feels less premium in hand ‚Äî though comfort on the head is competitive. For long daily wear, both are comfortable; the INZONE has a slight edge in light weight; the Nova Pro has a clear edge in perceived durability.

Microphone

The Nova Pro's bidirectional mic is among the best on any gaming headset ‚Äî Discord and streaming users consistently rate it highly. It's retractable (fold it up when not in use). The INZONE H9's beam-forming mic is good but slightly behind in voice isolation. If you stream, record, or frequently make calls, the Nova Pro mic is meaningfully better.

Our Verdict

For PC-first gamers, the Arctis Nova Pro Wireless is the better headset. The hot-swap battery system, simultaneous dual connection, and mic quality justify the premium. It's the headset you buy and stop thinking about.

For PS5-first gamers, the INZONE H9 is the correct choice ‚Äî Sony's spatial audio integration on PS5 is a real feature that makes a real difference in compatible titles, and the ¬£50 saving is meaningful.
