Logitech G Pro X 2 vs HyperX Alloy Origins

Quick Answer

The G Pro X 2 is one of the most acclaimed wireless TKL keyboards ever made. The HyperX Alloy Origins TKL is a full-aluminium wired TKL that costs almost half the price. Both target competitive gamers. The question is whether wireless is worth the premium.

Detailed Breakdown

Wireless vs Wired ‚Äî the core trade-off

LIGHTSPEED wireless is legitimately comparable to wired ‚Äî Logitech has proven this across years of pro adoption. You will not notice input lag. What you will notice is the freedom of no cable and the need to charge every ~50 hours. The Alloy Origins is wired but adds a USB passthrough port, which is actually useful on a desk where USB ports are occupied.

Build quality ‚Äî Alloy Origins has more aluminium

Both are built for competitive use. The Alloy Origins has a full aluminium body which is slightly more premium in terms of raw material. The G Pro X 2 has an aluminium top plate with a plastic base to save weight. Both feel solid and will survive competitive use. The Alloy Origins is noticeably heavier.

Polling rate ‚Äî G Pro X 2 has a meaningful edge

2000Hz polling on the G Pro X 2 means double the position updates per second compared to the standard 1000Hz on the Alloy Origins. This is a real difference at the highest competitive levels. For most gamers at sub-Radiant / sub-FACEIT Level 10, 1000Hz is perfectly adequate.

Switches ‚Äî both are solid linear options

GX Red and HyperX Red are both smooth linear switches suited to gaming. The GX Red has a slightly lighter actuation force. Neither requires switch comparison to be the deciding factor ‚Äî both will perform well in competitive use.

Our Verdict

The G Pro X 2 is the right choice specifically if wireless is your priority. LIGHTSPEED is genuinely excellent and if you're cable-averse, the premium is justified. Outside of that use case, the Alloy Origins is the smarter buy.
