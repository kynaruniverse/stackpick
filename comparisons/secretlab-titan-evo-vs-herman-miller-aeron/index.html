The Core Difference: Foam vs Mesh

The Secretlab uses high-density cold-cure foam throughout. It's firm initially and softens over months of use. It supports your weight well and doesn't bottom out. The limitation: foam retains heat and body shape over time. After 3â€“5 years of daily use, the foam compresses â€” the chair still works, but is less supportive.

The Aeron uses Herman Miller's 8Z Pellicle suspension mesh. It distributes your weight across the entire seat surface simultaneously, never retains heat (air flows through constantly), and doesn't compress or degrade over time. After 10 years of daily use, an Aeron feels essentially the same as when new. This is the core technical advantage at the higher price.

Lumbar Support

The TITAN Evo's L-ADAPT lumbar system adjusts in 4 directions and provides genuine lumbar support that most gaming chairs don't match. It's a real system, not just a pillow. However, it only addresses the lumbar (lower back) region.

The Aeron's PostureFit SL supports both the lumbar and sacral (tailbone) regions independently. This addresses the full S-curve of the spine â€” keeping the pelvis tilted forward, which naturally supports the entire spine. It's a fundamentally different approach that takes adjustment to get right, but once dialled in, it reduces lower back fatigue substantially more than lumbar-only solutions.
