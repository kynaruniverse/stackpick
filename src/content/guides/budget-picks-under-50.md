You don't need to spend £200+ to game well. These are the genuinely good picks under £50 — not compromises, but the right tools for the budget.

## What to Avoid Under £50

Membrane keyboards — they feel mushy and provide no tactile feedback. Avoid any keyboard described as quiet or silent that does not also say mechanical. RGB lighting on a bad keyboard is still a bad keyboard.

Fake gaming headsets from unknown brands with giant inflated specs (7.1 surround, 10Hz-40kHz frequency range) — these numbers are marketing, not audio quality. A real 50mm driver from a reputable brand will outperform any no-name brand at the same price.

Wireless mice under £25 from unknown brands — cheap Bluetooth gaming mice will have noticeable lag. Spend the £48 for the ATK VXE MAD R+ or buy a good wired mouse instead.
