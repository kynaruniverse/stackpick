Buying Guide

Mechanical vs Membrane

Mechanical keyboards use individual switches under each key. They're more durable (50-100 million keystrokes), offer better tactile feedback, and allow faster typing. All our recommendations are mechanical - they're the gold standard for gaming.

Membrane keyboards use rubber domes under a single membrane. They're quieter and cheaper but less responsive and durable. We don't recommend membrane keyboards for serious gaming.

Switch Types

Linear (Red): Smooth keypress with no tactile bump. Best for gaming, especially FPS. The Redragon K552 and Logitech G PRO X use linear switches.

Tactile (Brown): Small bump when pressed. Good all-rounder for gaming and typing.

Adjustable (OmniPoint): SteelSeries Apex Pro keyboards let you customize actuation point from 0.2mm to 3.8mm. Perfect for both gaming and typing.

Form Factors

Full-size (100%): All keys including numpad. Takes more desk space.

Tenkeyless (TKL / 80%): No numpad. More mouse room for low-sens gamers. Our Logitech and SteelSeries Apex Pro TKL are this size.

75%: Compact with arrow keys retained. The RK84 uses this efficient layout.

60%: Minimal design, no function row or arrows. The Redragon K552 and Apex Pro Mini save maximum desk space.

Wired vs Wireless

Modern wireless keyboards like the Logitech G PRO X TKL and SteelSeries Apex Pro have zero latency thanks to LIGHTSPEED and Quantum 2.0 wireless technology. Battery life ranges from 40+ hours, making them viable for competitive gaming.

Hot-Swappable Switches

The RK ROYAL KLUDGE RK84 features hot-swappable switches, letting you change switch types without soldering. Great for experimenting with different feels or replacing worn switches.

Our Testing Process

We test keyboards in competitive games like CS2, Valorant, and League of Legends. We measure actuation force, key travel distance, and polling rates. Each keyboard is used for at least 40 hours of gaming to assess comfort, durability, and wireless latency where applicable. We also test build quality and keycap durability.
