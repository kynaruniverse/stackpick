Buying Guide

Resolution

1080p (Full HD): Easiest to drive. The LG 24GS65F and BenQ ZOWIE XL2566X+ use 1080p for maximum frame rates in competitive games. Best for esports players who need 300+ FPS.

1440p (QHD): Sweet spot for gaming. The ASUS PG27AQDP delivers stunning 1440p at 480Hz - perfect balance of clarity and performance. Recommended for 27" screens.

4K (UHD): Maximum detail. The ASUS PG32UCDM offers 4K at 240Hz for both competitive and single-player gaming. Needs RTX 4080/4090 or RX 7900 XTX.

Ultrawide (5120Ã—1440): The Samsung Odyssey G9's 32:9 ratio provides immersive gaming with double the horizontal space of standard monitors.

Refresh Rate

144-180Hz: Minimum for gaming monitors. The LG 24GS65F's 180Hz delivers smooth motion for most gamers.

240Hz: High competitive standard. The Samsung G9 and ASUS PG32UCDM hit this mark.

360-480Hz: Elite esports tier. The BenQ ZOWIE (400Hz) and ASUS PG27AQDP (480Hz) offer the smoothest motion possible.

Panel Types

IPS: Best colors and viewing angles. The LG 24GS65F uses IPS for vibrant visuals at budget pricing.

Fast-TN: Fastest response times. The BenQ ZOWIE uses TN for competitive advantage, trading color quality for speed.

OLED (QD-OLED/WOLED): Premium tier. Perfect blacks, infinite contrast, 0.03ms response. The ASUS monitors use WOLED, Samsung uses QD-OLED. Both deliver stunning image quality but risk burn-in with static UI elements.

Response Time & Motion Clarity

OLED monitors (ASUS, Samsung) achieve 0.03ms GTG response - virtually instant. The BenQ ZOWIE adds DyAc 2 motion blur reduction for crystal-clear tracking in fast movement. Standard IPS like the LG achieves 1ms, which is excellent for budget gaming.

OLED Burn-In Protection

Modern OLED monitors include protection: ASUS uses OLED Care+ (pixel shifting, heatsink, firmware updates), Samsung uses panel refresh cycles. Avoid leaving static HUDs on screen for hours. Enable screensavers and taskbar auto-hide.

Size & Ergonomics

24-24.5": Esports standard (BenQ, LG). Entire screen visible without head movement.

27": Sweet spot for 1440p (ASUS PG27AQDP). Perfect pixel density and desk footprint.

32": Ideal for 4K (ASUS PG32UCDM). Larger screen shows 4K detail without scaling.

49" Ultrawide: Immersive wraparound (Samsung G9). Replaces dual-monitor setups.

Adaptive Sync

All our picks support G-SYNC Compatible and/or FreeSync Premium to eliminate screen tearing. Works with both NVIDIA and AMD GPUs.
