Buying Guide

Refresh Rate

Higher refresh rates (144Hz, 240Hz, 360Hz) provide smoother motion and lower input lag. 144Hz is the biggest jump from 60Hz and where most gamers should start. For competitive FPS, 240Hz helps but only if your PC can push 200+ fps consistently ‚Äî otherwise you won't feel the difference.

Resolution & Panel Type

1080p at 24" is sharp and the easiest to push high framerates on, ideal for competitive play. 1440p at 27" is the enthusiast sweet spot ‚Äî noticeably crisper without needing top-tier hardware. 4K suits 32"+ screens and needs a high-end GPU to hit playable framerates. For panel types: IPS gives the best colours and wide viewing angles, TN has the lowest latency, VA offers deeper blacks for dark game environments.

OLED: Is it Worth It?

OLED monitors deliver near-instant pixel response (0.03ms) and true blacks that IPS can't touch. The trade-off is cost and potential burn-in with static HUD elements over thousands of hours. For most gamers who play varied content, burn-in is not a realistic concern ‚Äî but it's worth knowing. The visual jump from a good IPS to OLED is genuinely impressive.

G-Sync vs FreeSync

Both eliminate screen tearing by syncing the monitor's refresh rate to your GPU's output. G-Sync Certified monitors have NVIDIA-validated hardware (usually adds ¬£50-100 to price). Most modern monitors are "G-Sync Compatible" ‚Äî meaning they pass NVIDIA's variable refresh rate testing and work fine with RTX cards via FreeSync. The difference is negligible for 95% of gamers.
